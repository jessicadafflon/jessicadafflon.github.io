Predicting Age from Neuroimaging using an Automated Machine Learning approach (autoML)
In neuroscience, the choice of the machine learning algorithm and its hyperparameters, are often arbitrary and frequently do not correspond to the statistical properties of the data being analysed. In this project, I used an autoML approach to explore a vast space of models and their hyperparameters, and in an entirely data-driven fashion, find the pipeline that most accurately predicts brain age.

A comparison between machine learning to predict brain age
Different algorithms have been used to predict brain age from structural images. However, every study uses different algorithms on different datasets, making it difficult to compare the results among each other. This project aimed to bridge this gap and rigorously compare the most commonly used algorithms to predict brain age and to compare the impact of using different inputs on the precision of the analysis.

An Embodied Simulation of the Human Brain
Although different computational models have been developed to investigate the neural dynamics of the brain, less is known about the brain-environment interaction and how the latter influence brain dynamics. Therefore, we developed a virtual agent, whose activity is driven by a computational model, and analysed how the environment influenced its neural dynamics.

Implementation of Large-scale Brain Simulations on NeuroML
This project aimed to implement Large-scale Brain Simulations, which are models that simulate the interaction between different brain areas, in NeuroML. NeuroML is a tool that standardises commonly used parameters to describe neuroscience models.
